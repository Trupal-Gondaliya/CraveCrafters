Cold drink Recipes

Cold Coffee

For the Cold Coffee Ingredients:

1 cup strong brewed coffee, cooled to room temperature
1 cup milk (dairy or non-dairy)
2-3 tablespoons granulated sugar (adjust to taste)
Ice cubes
Whipped cream (optional, for garnish)
Chocolate syrup or cocoa powder (optional, for garnish)

For the Cold Coffee Recipe:
Brew Coffee:
Prepare strong coffee using your preferred method (drip, French press, etc.). You can use regular coffee or espresso, depending on your preference for strength.

Cool Coffee:
Allow the brewed coffee to cool to room temperature. You can speed up the cooling process by placing it in the refrigerator for a few minutes.

Prepare Sweetened Coffee Base:
In a pitcher or large glass, combine the cooled coffee with granulated sugar. Stir until the sugar is completely dissolved.

Assemble Cold Coffee:
Fill serving glasses with ice cubes. Pour the sweetened coffee over the ice cubes, filling each glass about halfway.

Add Milk:
Pour milk over the coffee mixture, filling the glasses almost to the top. Stir gently to combine.

Garnish (Optional):
Top with a dollop of whipped cream if desired. Drizzle chocolate syrup or dust with cocoa powder on top for extra flavor and presentation.

Serve:
Serve cold coffee immediately with a straw and enjoy!

Mango ThickShake

For the Mango Thick Shake Ingredients:

2 ripe mangoes, peeled and chopped (about 2 cups)
1 cup cold milk (dairy or non-dairy)
1/2 cup vanilla ice cream
2 tablespoons granulated sugar, or to taste (optional, depending on the sweetness of mangoes)
Ice cubes (optional, for a colder shake)

For the Mango ThickShake Recipe:
Prepare Mangoes:
Peel the mangoes and cut the flesh away from the pit. Chop the mango flesh into chunks.

Blend Ingredients:
In a blender, combine the chopped mangoes, cold milk, vanilla ice cream, and granulated sugar (if using). Blend until smooth and creamy. Taste and adjust sweetness by adding more sugar if needed.

Adjust Consistency:
If the shake is too thick, add a little more milk and blend again until desired consistency is reached. If it's too thin, you can add more mango chunks or ice cream.

Serve:
Pour the mango thickshake into glasses. Optionally, garnish with a slice of mango or a sprinkle of ground cardamom for extra flavor.

Enjoy:
Serve immediately and enjoy the refreshing and creamy mango thickshake!

Strawberry MilkShake

For the Strawberry milkShake Ingredients:

2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and halved
2 cups vanilla ice cream
1 cup milk (dairy or non-dairy)
2 tablespoons granulated sugar, or to taste (optional, depending on the sweetness of strawberries)
Whipped cream, for topping (optional)
Fresh strawberries, for garnish (optional)

For the Strawberry MilkShake Recipe:
Prepare Strawberries:
Wash the strawberries thoroughly, hull them, and cut them in half.

Blend Ingredients:
In a blender, combine the fresh strawberries, vanilla ice cream, milk, and granulated sugar (if using). Blend until smooth and creamy. Taste and adjust sweetness by adding more sugar if needed.

Adjust Consistency:
If the milkshake is too thick, add a little more milk and blend again until desired consistency is reached. If it's too thin, you can add more strawberries or ice cream.

Serve:
Pour the strawberry milkshake into glasses. Optionally, top with whipped cream and garnish with a fresh strawberry or a sprinkle of chopped strawberries for extra flavor and presentation.

Enjoy:
Serve immediately and enjoy the refreshing and creamy strawberry milkshake!

Kaju Anjeer Shake

For the Kaju Anjeer Shake Ingredients:

1/2 cup cashew nuts (kaju), soaked in water for 4-6 hours or overnight
4-5 dried figs (anjeer), soaked in warm water for 1 hour
2 cups milk (dairy or non-dairy)
2-3 tablespoons honey or sugar, adjust to taste
1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder (optional)
Ice cubes (optional)

For the Kaju Anjeer Shake Recipe:
Prepare Cashew Nuts and Figs:
Drain the soaked cashew nuts and rinse them under cold water. Remove the stems from the soaked figs and roughly chop them.

Blend Ingredients:
In a blender, combine the soaked cashew nuts, soaked figs, milk, honey or sugar, and cardamom powder (if using). Blend until smooth and creamy. If the shake is too thick, add a little more milk until desired consistency is achieved.

Adjust Sweetness:
Taste the shake and adjust sweetness by adding more honey or sugar if needed. Blend again briefly to incorporate.

Serve:
Pour the kaju anjeer shake into glasses. Optionally, add a few ice cubes for a colder shake.

Garnish (Optional):
Garnish with a sprinkle of chopped cashews or a dusting of cardamom powder on top for extra flavor and presentation.

Enjoy:
Serve immediately and enjoy the rich and nutty flavors of kaju anjeer shake!

Watermelon Juice

For the Watermelon Juice Ingredients:

4 cups cubed seedless watermelon (about 1 small watermelon)
1-2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice (optional, for a tangy twist)
Ice cubes (optional, for serving)

For the Watermelon Juice Recipe:
Prepare the Watermelon:
Cut the watermelon into cubes, removing any seeds if necessary. Use seedless watermelon for convenience.

Blend the Watermelon:
In a blender, add the cubed watermelon pieces. If desired, squeeze in fresh lemon juice for a tangy flavor.

Blend Until Smooth:
Blend the watermelon cubes until smooth and liquidy. If you prefer a smoother juice, you can strain the mixture through a fine mesh sieve to remove pulp.

Serve Chilled:
Pour the watermelon juice into glasses over ice cubes, if using.

Garnish (Optional):
Garnish with a slice of watermelon or a sprig of mint for a fresh touch.

Enjoy:
Serve immediately and enjoy the refreshing taste of homemade watermelon juice!

Kiwi Juice

For the Kiwi Juice Ingredients:

4-5 ripe kiwifruits, peeled and chopped
1-2 tablespoons honey or sugar (optional, adjust to taste)
1 cup cold water
Ice cubes (optional, for serving)

For the Kiwi Juice Recipe:
Prepare the Kiwifruits:
Peel the kiwifruits using a knife or vegetable peeler. Cut them into chunks.

Blend the Kiwi:
In a blender, add the chopped kiwifruits and honey or sugar (if using). Add cold water to the blender.

Blend Until Smooth:
Blend everything until smooth and well combined. If you prefer a smoother juice, strain the mixture through a fine mesh sieve to remove any pulp.

Serve Chilled:
Pour the kiwi juice into glasses over ice cubes, if desired.

Garnish (Optional):
Garnish with a slice of kiwifruit or a sprig of mint for a fresh presentation.

Enjoy:
Serve immediately and enjoy the tangy and refreshing taste of homemade kiwi juice!
