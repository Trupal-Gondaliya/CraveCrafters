Kashmiri Recipes

Dum Aloo

For the Dum Aloo Ingredients:
For the Gravy:
10-12 baby potatoes, boiled and peeled
2 tablespoons oil or ghee
1 onion, finely chopped
2 tomatoes, pureed
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 bay leaf
1-inch cinnamon stick
2-3 green cardamom pods
2-3 cloves
1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder (adjust to taste)
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1/2 teaspoon garam masala
Salt to taste
1/2 cup yogurt, whisked
1/2 cup water or as needed
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
For Garnish (Optional):
Fresh cream
Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)

For the Dum aLoo Recipe:
Boiling and Preparing Potatoes:
Wash the baby potatoes thoroughly. Boil them until they are just tender. Once cooled, peel them and prick them with a fork or toothpick all over. This helps the potatoes absorb the flavors of the gravy.

Making the Gravy:
Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan or kadhai over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon stick, green cardamom pods, and cloves. Sauté for a few seconds until they become aromatic. Add finely chopped onion and sauté until it turns golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears. Add Kashmiri red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook the spices for a minute. Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala mixture.

Cooking the Potatoes:
Lower the heat and add whisked yogurt. Stir continuously to avoid curdling. Add water as needed to adjust the consistency of the gravy. Add boiled and peeled baby potatoes to the gravy. Mix gently to coat the potatoes with the masala. Cover the pan with a lid and simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes absorb the flavors of the gravy.

Finishing Touches:
Add garam masala and mix well. Adjust salt and spice levels according to your taste. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Optional: Drizzle some fresh cream and sprinkle kasuri methi (crushed between palms) for added flavor.

Serving:
Serve Dum Aloo hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice. Enjoy the rich and aromatic flavors of this classic Indian dish!

Kashmiri Pulao

For the Kashmiri Pulao Ingredients:

1 cup basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes and drained
1 tablespoon ghee or oil
1 tablespoon oil
1 onion, thinly sliced
1/4 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans), finely chopped
10-12 cashew nuts
10-12 almonds
10-12 raisins
2 tablespoons fresh grated coconut (optional)
1 bay leaf
2-3 green cardamom pods
4-5 cloves
1-inch cinnamon stick
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
2 cups water or vegetable broth
Salt to taste
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
For Garnish (Optional):
Fried onions
Saffron milk (a pinch of saffron soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk)

For the Kashmiri Pulao Recipe:
Preparing the Pulao:
Heat ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pan or kadhai over medium heat. Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Add mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans), cashew nuts, almonds, raisins, and fresh grated coconut (if using). Sauté for a few minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender and nuts are lightly toasted.

Adding Spices:
Add bay leaf, green cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, and cumin seeds. Sauté for a minute until aromatic.

Cooking the Rice:
Add soaked and drained basmati rice to the pan. Stir gently to coat the rice with the spices and vegetables. Pour 2 cups of water or vegetable broth into the pan. Season with salt according to taste. Bring the mixture to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid, and simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the rice is cooked and water is absorbed. Fluff the rice gently with a fork.

Garnishing and Serving:
Garnish Kashmiri Pulao with chopped fresh coriander leaves and fried onions. Serve:
Serve hot Kashmiri Pulao as a main dish or as an accompaniment to any curry of your choice.

Rajma

For the Rajma Ingredients:

1 cup rajma (kidney beans), soaked overnight or for at least 6-8 hours
2 tablespoons ghee or oil
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 onion, finely chopped
2 tomatoes, pureed
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder (adjust to taste)
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1/2 teaspoon fennel powder (saunf powder)
1/2 teaspoon dry ginger powder (saunth)
1/2 teaspoon garam masala
Salt to taste
2 cups water or as needed
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

For the Rajma Recipe:
Preparing Rajma:
Rinse the soaked rajma under running water until the water runs clear. Pressure cook rajma with enough water (about 3 cups) and a pinch of salt until they are tender. This usually takes about 15-20 minutes after the cooker reaches full pressure. You can also cook them in a pot until tender if you don't have a pressure cooker.

Making Kashmiri Rajma:
Heat ghee or oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.

Adding Spices:
Add Kashmiri red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, fennel powder, dry ginger powder, and garam masala. Mix well and cook the spices for a minute until fragrant.

Cooking the Curry:
Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala mixture. Add cooked rajma along with its cooking water (or fresh water as needed). Mix well. Season with salt according to taste. Bring the curry to a boil.

Simmering:
Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan with a lid, and simmer the Kashmiri Rajma for 15-20 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld together and the rajma to absorb the spices.

Garnishing and Serving:
Garnish with chopped fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot Kashmiri Rajma with steamed rice or roti. It pairs well with a side of plain yogurt or salad.
