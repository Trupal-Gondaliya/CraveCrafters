South Indian Recipes

Idli

For the Idli Ingredients:

1 cup parboiled rice (idli rice)
1 cup whole or split urad dal (black gram dal)
Water, as needed for soaking and grinding
Salt, to taste

For the Idli Recipe:
Preparation:
Wash the rice and urad dal separately a few times under running water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice and urad dal separately in enough water. Let them soak for at least 4-6 hours, or overnight.

Grinding:
Drain the water from the soaked urad dal. Grind it in a blender or wet grinder with a little water until it becomes a smooth and fluffy batter. This may take about 10-15 minutes of grinding, scraping the sides as needed. Transfer the ground urad dal batter into a large bowl. Drain the water from the soaked rice and grind it to a slightly coarse texture, adding water as needed. The rice batter should be smooth yet slightly grainy. Combine the rice batter with the urad dal batter in the bowl. Mix well using your hands or a spoon to incorporate both batters thoroughly. Add salt to taste and mix well again.

Fermentation:
Cover the bowl with a lid or a cloth and let the batter ferment in a warm place for about 8-12 hours or overnight. The batter should rise and become fluffy.

Steaming:
Grease the idli molds or plates with oil or ghee. Mix the batter gently. Pour spoonfuls of the batter into each mold, filling them almost to the top. Place the idli stand in a steamer or a large pot with boiling water. Steam the idlis on medium-high heat for about 10-12 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the idlis comes out clean. Once done, remove the idli stand from the steamer and let it cool for a few minutes.

Serving:
Carefully remove the idlis from the molds using a spoon or knife. Serve hot idlis with sambar, coconut chutney, or any chutney of your choice.

Dosa

For the Dosa Ingredients:

1 cup parboiled rice (idli rice)
1/2 cup whole or split urad dal (black gram dal)
1/4 cup flattened rice (poha)
Water, as needed for soaking and grinding
Salt, to taste
Oil or ghee, for cooking dosas

For the Dosa Recipe:
Preparation:
Wash the rice, urad dal, and flattened rice (poha) separately under running water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice and urad dal together in enough water. Soak the flattened rice (poha) separately. Let them soak for at least 4-6 hours, or overnight.

Grinding:
Drain the water from the soaked urad dal. Grind it in a blender or wet grinder with a little water until it becomes a smooth and fluffy batter. This may take about 10-15 minutes of grinding, scraping the sides as needed. Transfer the ground urad dal batter into a large bowl. Drain the water from the soaked rice and flattened rice (poha). Grind them to a smooth batter, adding water as needed. The batter should be slightly coarse yet smooth. Combine the rice and flattened rice batter with the urad dal batter in the bowl. Mix well using your hands or a spoon to incorporate both batters thoroughly. Add salt to taste and mix well again.

Fermentation:
Cover the bowl with a lid or a cloth and let the batter ferment in a warm place for about 8-12 hours or overnight. The batter should rise and become slightly bubbly.

Making Dosas:
After fermentation, mix the batter gently. Heat a non-stick or cast-iron dosa tava (griddle) over medium-high heat. Once hot, reduce the heat to medium. Grease the tava lightly with oil or ghee. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the tava. Quickly spread the batter in a circular motion using the back of the ladle to form a thin, even layer. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa and on top if desired. Cook the dosa until the edges start to brown and crisp up, and the surface looks cooked (about 1-2 minutes). Flip the dosa carefully using a spatula and cook the other side for another 1-2 minutes, or until it is cooked through and lightly golden brown.

Serving:
Fold the dosa in half or roll it up and serve hot with sambar, coconut chutney, or any chutney of your choice.

Coconut Chutney

For the Coconut Chutney Ingredients:

1 cup fresh grated coconut (you can use frozen grated coconut as well)
2-3 green chilies (adjust according to your spice preference)
1/2 inch piece of ginger, peeled
1/4 cup roasted chana dal (split roasted gram dal)
A small handful of fresh coriander leaves (optional)
1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice or tamarind pulp (optional, for tanginess)
Salt, to taste
Water, as needed
For Tempering (Optional):
1 tablespoon oil (preferably coconut oil)
1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
1/2 teaspoon urad dal (black gram dal)
A few curry leaves

For the Coconut Chutney Recipe:
Grind the Chutney:
In a blender or food processor, add grated coconut, green chilies, ginger, roasted chana dal, coriander leaves (if using), lemon juice or tamarind pulp (if using), and salt. Add a little water to facilitate grinding and blend until you get a smooth paste. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if needed.

Tempering (Optional):
Heat oil in a small pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add urad dal and sauté until it turns golden brown. Add curry leaves and sauté for a few seconds until they become crisp. Pour the tempering over the prepared coconut chutney and mix well.

Serve:
Transfer the coconut chutney to a serving bowl. Serve it as a side dish with dosas, idlis, vadas, or any South Indian dish of your choice.

Medu Vada

For the Meduvada Ingredients:

1 cup urad dal (split black gram dal)
2-3 tablespoons rice flour (optional, for crispiness)
1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
1 inch piece of ginger, grated
A small bunch of fresh coriander leaves, chopped
Salt, to taste
Oil, for deep frying

For the Meduvada Recipe:
Preparing the Batter:
Wash and soak urad dal in water for 4-6 hours or overnight. Drain the soaked dal and grind it into a smooth batter using minimal water. The batter should be thick and fluffy. Transfer the batter to a bowl and add rice flour (if using), chopped green chilies, grated ginger, chopped coriander leaves, and salt. Mix well to combine.

Shaping and Frying:
Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium heat. Wet your hands with water, take a small portion of batter, and shape it into a smooth round ball. Flatten slightly to form a vada shape with a hole in the center. Gently slide the shaped vada into the hot oil, taking care not to overcrowd the pan. Fry the vadas on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crispy on both sides. Remove the fried vadas using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.

Serve:
Serve hot Medu Vadas with coconut chutney and sambar.

Sambhar

For the Sambhar Ingredients:

1/2 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
1 small lemon-sized ball of tamarind
1 cup mixed vegetables (such as carrots, potatoes, drumsticks, beans, pumpkin, etc.), chopped
2 tomatoes, chopped
1 onion, chopped
2-3 green chilies, slit
1 teaspoon sambar powder (adjust to taste)
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder

Salt, to taste
Water, as needed
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
For Tempering:
2 tablespoons oil
1 teaspoon mustard seeds
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
Few curry leaves

For the Sambhar Recipe:
Cooking the Dal:
Wash and pressure cook toor dal with enough water until soft and mushy. Mash the cooked dal and set aside.

Preparing Tamarind Extract:
Soak tamarind in warm water for 15-20 minutes. Squeeze to extract the pulp. Discard the pulp and keep the extract.

Cooking Sambar:
In a large pot or pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add cumin seeds, hing, and curry leaves. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent. Add chopped tomatoes, green chilies, and cook until tomatoes turn mushy. Add mixed vegetables, turmeric powder, sambar powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes. Add tamarind extract and bring to a boil. Cook until vegetables are tender.

Adding Dal and Finishing:
Add mashed dal to the pot and mix well. Adjust consistency with water if needed. Simmer the sambar on low heat for 8-10 minutes, allowing flavors to blend.

Tempering:
Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a small pan for tempering. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add curry leaves and pour the tempering over the sambar.

Serve:
Garnish with chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot sambar with dosas, idlis, vadas, or rice.

Uttapam

For the Uttapam Ingredients:

2 cups dosa batter (fermented rice and lentil batter, available at Indian grocery stores or homemade)
1 small onion, finely chopped
1 small tomato, finely chopped
1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
Salt, to taste
Oil or ghee, for cooking

For the Uttapam Recipe:
Preparation:
Ensure the dosa batter is well fermented. If it's too thick, add a little water to achieve a thick pouring consistency. Heat a non-stick pan or a cast iron skillet (tava) on medium heat. Grease it lightly with oil or ghee.

Making Uttapam:
Take a ladleful of dosa batter and pour it onto the center of the heated pan. Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a circular motion to make a thick pancake-like shape, about 5-6 inches in diameter.

Adding Toppings:
Immediately sprinkle chopped onions, tomatoes, green chilies (if using), and coriander leaves evenly over the Uttapam. Lightly press the toppings into the batter with the back of the ladle.

Cooking:
Drizzle a few drops of oil or ghee around the edges of the Uttapam. Cover and cook on medium-low heat for 2-3 minutes, or until the bottom turns golden brown and crisp.

Flipping:
Gently flip the Uttapam using a spatula. Cook the other side uncovered for another 2-3 minutes, or until it is cooked through.

Serve:
Once both sides are cooked and golden brown, transfer the Uttapam to a plate. Serve hot with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, or sambar.
