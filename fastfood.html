Fast Food Recipes

Burger

For the Burger Ingredients:
For the veggie patties:

2 cups cooked chickpeas or black beans (or a mix of both)
1 cup breadcrumbs
1/2 cup grated carrots
1/2 cup finely chopped onions
1/4 cup finely chopped bell peppers
2 cloves garlic, minced
2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley or cilantro
1 tablespoon soy sauce
1 tablespoon tomato paste or ketchup
1 teaspoon ground cumin
1 teaspoon paprika
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon black pepper
1-2 tablespoons olive oil (for cooking)
For assembling the burgers:
Burger buns
Lettuce leaves
Tomato slices
Onion slices
Pickles
Cheese slices (optional)
Ketchup, mustard, or your favorite burger sauce

For the Burger Recipe:
Preparing the Patties:
In a large mixing bowl, mash the chickpeas or black beans with a fork or potato masher until mostly smooth, leaving some chunks for texture. Add the breadcrumbs, grated carrots, chopped onions, bell peppers, minced garlic, parsley or cilantro, soy sauce, tomato paste or ketchup, cumin, paprika, salt, and black pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are thoroughly combined. If the mixture is too dry, add a little water or more tomato paste/ketchup. If it's too wet, add more breadcrumbs. Divide the mixture into equal portions and shape them into patties.

Cooking the Patties:
Heat the olive oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Cook the patties for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and crispy on the outside. Be gentle when flipping them to avoid breaking.

Assembling the Burgers:
Lightly toast the burger buns on a skillet or in the oven. Place a lettuce leaf on the bottom half of each bun. Add the cooked veggie patty on top of the lettuce. Layer with tomato slices, onion slices, pickles, and cheese slices (if using). Spread ketchup, mustard, or your favorite burger sauce on the top half of the bun. Place the top bun over the assembled burger.

Serving:
Serve the veggie burgers warm with a side of fries, salad, or your favorite side dish.

Fries

For the Fries Ingredients:

4 large potatoes
2-3 tablespoons vegetable oil
Salt, to taste
Optional seasonings: paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, black pepper, dried herbs (like rosemary or thyme)

For the Fries Recipe:
Preparation:
Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or lightly grease it with oil.

Cutting the Potatoes:
Wash and peel the potatoes (you can leave the skin on if you prefer). Cut the potatoes into even-sized sticks, about 1/4 inch thick.

Soaking the Potatoes:
Place the cut potatoes in a bowl of cold water and let them soak for at least 30 minutes. This helps remove excess starch and makes the fries crispier. After soaking, drain the potatoes and pat them dry with a clean kitchen towel or paper towels.

Seasoning the Potatoes:
In a large bowl, toss the dried potato sticks with vegetable oil, making sure they are evenly coated. Add salt and any optional seasonings you like, and toss again to coat evenly.

Baking the Fries:
Spread the potato sticks in a single layer on the prepared baking sheet. Make sure they are not touching each other for even cooking. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, flipping the fries halfway through, until they are golden brown and crispy.

Serving:
Once baked, remove the fries from the oven and let them cool slightly. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. Serve the fries hot with ketchup, mayonnaise, or your favorite dipping sauce.

Frenky

For the Frenky Ingredients:
For Dough:
1 cup whole wheat flour (atta)
Water, as needed
Salt, to taste
For Potato Filling:
3-4 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
1 onion, finely chopped
1 green chili, finely chopped (optional)
1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
Salt, to taste
1 tablespoon oil
For Cheese Filling:
Grated cheese (like mozzarella or cheddar), as needed
Chopped fresh coriander leaves (cilantro), optional
For Assembly:
Oil or ghee, for cooking
Tomato ketchup or chutney, as desired

For the Pizza Recipe:
Prepare the Dough:
In a mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead into a smooth dough. Cover the dough and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.

Prepare the Potato Filling:
Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and green chili (if using). Sauté until onions turn translucent. Add turmeric powder, garam masala powder, and salt. Mix well. Add mashed potatoes and mix everything together. Cook for 2-3 minutes until flavors combine. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

Assemble the Frankie:
Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls. Roll each ball into a thin circle (like a chapati) using a rolling pin. Heat a tawa or griddle over medium heat. Cook the rolled dough circle on both sides until light golden brown spots appear. Remove and keep aside. Place a cooked dough circle on a clean surface. Spread some tomato ketchup or chutney over it (optional). Place a portion of the potato filling in the center of the dough circle. Sprinkle some grated cheese and chopped coriander leaves over the filling. Fold the sides of the dough circle over the filling, covering it completely to form a roll. Heat a little oil or ghee on the tawa or griddle. Place the assembled Frankie seam-side down and cook until golden brown and crisp on both sides. Repeat for remaining Frankies.

Serve:
Once cooked, cut each Frankie into halves or serve whole. Serve hot with additional tomato ketchup or chutney on the side.

Veg Tacos

For the Tacos Ingredients:
For the filling:

1 tablespoon olive oil
1 medium onion, finely chopped
2 cloves garlic, minced
1 bell pepper, finely chopped
1 zucchini, finely chopped
1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
1 can black beans, rinsed and drained
1 tablespoon taco seasoning (store-bought or homemade)
Salt and pepper, to taste
For the toppings:
Shredded lettuce
Diced tomatoes
Diced avocado or guacamole
Shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or your favorite)
Sour cream or yogurt
Salsa or pico de gallo
Chopped fresh cilantro
Lime wedges
For the tortillas:
8 small corn or flour tortillas

For the Tacos Recipe:
Preparing the Filling:
Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until they are soft and fragrant, about 2-3 minutes. Add the bell pepper and zucchini, and cook for another 5 minutes until they are tender. Stir in the corn, black beans, and taco seasoning. Cook for another 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until everything is well combined and heated through. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove from heat and set aside.

Preparing the Tortillas:
Warm the tortillas in a dry skillet over medium heat for about 30 seconds on each side, or until they are soft and pliable. You can also wrap them in a damp paper towel and microwave for about 30 seconds.

Assembling the Tacos:
Place a few spoonfuls of the vegetable filling in the center of each tortilla. Top with shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, avocado or guacamole, shredded cheese, sour cream or yogurt, salsa or pico de gallo, and chopped cilantro as desired. Squeeze a lime wedge over the top for a burst of fresh flavor.

Serving:
Serve the tacos immediately with extra lime wedges and any additional toppings you like.

Tomato Pasta

For the Tomato Pasta Ingredients:

12 oz (340 g) pasta (spaghetti, penne, or your favorite type)
2 tablespoons olive oil
1 medium onion, finely chopped
3-4 cloves garlic, minced
1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes or 4 cups fresh tomatoes, chopped
1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
1 teaspoon dried oregano
1 teaspoon dried basil
Salt and pepper, to taste
1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for a bit of heat)
Fresh basil leaves, chopped (for garnish)
Grated Parmesan cheese (for serving, optional)

For the Tomato Pasta Recipe:
Cook the Pasta:
Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water and then drain the pasta. Set aside.

Prepare the Tomato Sauce:
Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent, about 5-7 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Add the crushed tomatoes (or fresh tomatoes), dried oregano, dried basil, and red pepper flakes if using. Stir to combine. Bring the sauce to a simmer and let it cook for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly. If the sauce becomes too thick, you can add some of the reserved pasta water to reach your desired consistency. Season the sauce with sugar (if using), salt, and pepper to taste.

Combine Pasta and Sauce:
Add the cooked pasta to the skillet with the tomato sauce. Toss to coat the pasta evenly with the sauce. If needed, add more reserved pasta water to loosen the sauce and help it cling to the pasta.

Serving:
Divide the pasta among serving plates. Garnish with chopped fresh basil leaves and grated Parmesan cheese if desired. Serve immediately with a side of garlic bread or a simple salad.

Potato veg Sandwich

For the Potato Veg Sandwich Ingredients:
For the potato filling:

2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
1 tablespoon oil
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 small onion, finely chopped
1 green chili, finely chopped (optional)
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
1/2 teaspoon garam masala
Salt, to taste
1/4 cup peas (optional, boiled)
2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
For assembling the sandwich:
8 slices of bread (whole wheat, white, or your favorite type)
Butter or margarine, softened
Green chutney (optional)
Ketchup or tomato sauce (optional)
ed vegetables (cucumber, tomato, onion, bell pepper)
tuce leaves (optional)
Cheese slices (optional)

For the Potato Veg Sandwich Recipe:
Prepare the Potato Filling:
Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add finely chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent. Add green chili, if using, and sauté for another minute. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well. Add the mashed potatoes and peas (if using). Mix thoroughly until all the spices are well combined with the potatoes. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add chopped cilantro, mix well, and remove from heat. Let the mixture cool slightly.

Assemble the Sandwich:
Take two slices of bread. Spread butter on one side of each slice. Spread green chutney on one slice (buttered side) and ketchup or tomato sauce on the other slice (buttered side). Spread a generous amount of the potato filling on one slice. Top with sliced vegetables (cucumber, tomato, onion, bell pepper) and lettuce leaves, if using. Add a cheese slice on top of the vegetables if desired. Place the other slice of bread on top, buttered side down.

Grill or Toast the Sandwich:
Heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat. Add a little butter or oil to the pan. Place the sandwich on the skillet and cook until the bread is golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes. Flip the sandwich and cook the other side until golden brown and the cheese is melted, if using. Alternatively, you can use a sandwich maker or panini press to grill the sandwich.

Serve:
Remove the sandwich from the skillet and let it cool for a minute. Cut the sandwich in half diagonally and serve with extra green chutney, ketchup, or your favorite dipping sauce.

Pizza

For the Pizza Ingredients:
For the pizza dough:

2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast (1 packet)
1 1/2 cups warm water (110°F or 45°C)
3 1/2 to 4 cups all-purpose flour
2 tablespoons olive oil
2 teaspoons sugar
1 teaspoon salt
For the pizza sauce:
1 cup tomato sauce or pizza sauce
1 teaspoon dried oregano
1 teaspoon dried basil
1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
Salt and pepper to taste
For the topping:
2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
Olive oil for brushing

For the Pizza Recipe:
Making the Dough:
In a small bowl, dissolve the sugar in warm water and sprinkle the yeast over the top. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until frothy. In a large mixing bowl, combine 3 1/2 cups of flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Mix until a dough forms. If the dough is too sticky, add more flour a little at a time until it is manageable. Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-1.5 hours or until doubled in size.

Preparing the Sauce:
In a bowl, mix together the tomato sauce, dried oregano, dried basil, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Adjust the seasoning to taste.

Assembling the Pizza:
Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C) and place a pizza stone or an inverted baking sheet inside to heat. Punch down the risen dough and divide it into two equal portions for two pizzas or keep it whole for one large pizza. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to your desired thickness. Transfer the rolled-out dough to a piece of parchment paper for easy transfer to the oven. Spread a thin layer of the prepared pizza sauce over the dough, leaving a small border for the crust. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese evenly over the sauce.

Baking the Pizza:
Carefully transfer the pizza (with the parchment paper) onto the preheated pizza stone or baking sheet. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing.

Serving:
Brush the crust with a little olive oil if desired, slice, and serve your delicious homemade plain cheese pizza!

Veg Momos

For the Veg Momos Ingredients:
For the dough:
2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
1/2 teaspoon salt
Water, as needed
For the filling:
2 cups finely chopped cabbage
1/2 cup grated carrots
1/2 cup finely chopped bell peppers (capsicum)
1/4 cup finely chopped spring onions (green onions)
2-3 cloves garlic, minced
1-inch piece of ginger, grated or minced
1 tablespoon soy sauce
1 tablespoon vegetable oil
1 teaspoon sesame oil (optional)
Salt and pepper, to taste
Other ingredients:
Oil, for greasing and steaming
For dipping sauce (optional):
Soy sauce
Vinegar
Chili sauce or chopped fresh chilies (adjust to taste)
Chopped cilantro (optional)

For the Veg Momos Recipe:
Prepare the Dough:
In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt. Gradually add water, a little at a time, and knead until you get a smooth and firm dough. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

Prepare the Filling:
Heat vegetable oil (and sesame oil if using) in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add minced garlic and grated ginger. Sauté for a minute until fragrant. Add chopped cabbage, grated carrots, bell peppers, and spring onions. Stir-fry for 5-6 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp. Add soy sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and let the filling cool completely.

Assemble the Momos:
Divide the rested dough into small lemon-sized balls and roll each ball into a smooth ball. Roll out each ball into a thin circle of about 3-4 inches in diameter, using a rolling pin and a lightly floured surface. Place a spoonful of the cooled vegetable filling in the center of each circle.

Shape the Momos:
To shape the momos, start pleating and folding the edges of the circle towards the center, forming small pleats as you go around. Pinch the edges together to seal the momo. You can also create different shapes like half-moon or crescent shapes. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling to make more momos.

Steam the Momos:
Grease a steamer basket or line it with cabbage leaves to prevent sticking. Arrange the momos in the steamer basket, leaving some space between them to expand during steaming. Steam the momos over boiling water for about 10-12 minutes, or until the dough is cooked through and the filling is hot and cooked.

Serve:
Serve the hot momos immediately with a dipping sauce made of soy sauce mixed with vinegar, chili sauce or chopped fresh chilies, and chopped cilantro if desired. Enjoy your delicious vegetarian momos as a snack or appetizer!

Pani Puri

For the Pani Puri Ingredients:
For the Pani (Spicy Tangy Water):
1/2 cup tamarind pulp (you can soak tamarind in warm water and extract pulp)
1/4 cup mint leaves
1/4 cup cilantro (coriander) leaves
1-2 green chilies (adjust to taste)
1-inch piece of ginger, peeled
1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
1 teaspoon chaat masala
1/2 teaspoon black salt (kala namak)
1/2 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
4 cups cold water
For the Puri (Crispy Shells):
1 cup semolina (sooji/rava)
1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
Water, as needed
Oil, for deep frying
For the Filling:
1 cup boiled and mashed potatoes
1/2 cup boiled chickpeas (optional)
Chaat masala, to taste
Salt, to taste
Tamarind chutney (optional)
Green chutney (optional)
Sev (crunchy chickpea flour noodles, optional)
Finely chopped onions (optional)

For the Pani Puri Recipe:
Prepare the Pani:
In a blender or food processor, combine tamarind pulp, mint leaves, cilantro leaves, green chilies, and ginger. Blend until smooth. Strain the mixture using a fine mesh sieve into a large bowl to remove any solids. Add roasted cumin powder, chaat masala, black salt, regular salt, and cold water to the strained mixture. Mix well and adjust seasoning according to taste. Chill the pani in the refrigerator until ready to use.

Prepare the Puri (Crispy Shells):
In a mixing bowl, combine semolina and all-purpose flour. Gradually add water and knead into a stiff dough. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 20-30 minutes. Divide the dough into small balls and roll each ball into a thin circle (about 2 inches in diameter). Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium heat. Fry the rolled circles of dough until they puff up and turn golden brown. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil. Repeat with remaining dough.

Prepare the Filling:
In a bowl, mix boiled and mashed potatoes with chaat masala and salt. Adjust seasoning to taste. Optionally, mix in boiled chickpeas for added texture.

Assemble the Pani Puri:
Make a small hole in the center of each puri by tapping it gently with your thumb or using a spoon handle. Fill each puri with a spoonful of potato filling and chickpeas (if using). Dip each filled puri into the chilled pani and serve immediately. Optionally, garnish with tamarind chutney, green chutney, sev, and finely chopped onions.

Serve:
Serve Pani Puri immediately after assembling, as the puris tend to get soggy if left for too long.
