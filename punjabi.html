Dal Makhni

For the Dal Makhni Ingredients:
For the Dal:
1 cup whole black lentils (urad dal)
1/4 cup red kidney beans (rajma)
4 cups water
1-inch ginger, chopped
1 tablespoon garlic, chopped
For the Gravy:
2 tablespoons butter
1 tablespoon oil
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 medium onion, finely chopped
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
2-3 green chilies, slit
2 medium tomatoes, pureed
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1 teaspoon garam masala
Salt, to taste
1 cup fresh cream
1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish

For the Dal Makhnni Recipe:
Soaking and Boiling the Lentils:
Rinse the black lentils and kidney beans thoroughly. Soak them in enough water for at least 8 hours or overnight. Drain and transfer them to a pressure cooker. Add 4 cups of water, chopped ginger, and garlic. Pressure cook for 15-20 minutes or until the lentils and beans are soft and cooked through.

Preparing the Gravy:
In a large pan, heat the butter and oil over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for another 2-3 minutes. Add the pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil starts to separate from the mixture. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.

Combining Lentils with Gravy:
Add the cooked lentils and beans to the gravy. Mix well. Simmer on low heat for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add water if the dal thickens too much.

Finishing Touches:
Stir in the fresh cream and mix well. Crush the kasuri methi between your palms and add it to the dal. Cook for another 5-10 minutes, adjusting the seasoning as needed.

Serving:
Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.

Chole

For the Chole Ingredients:
For Cooking Chickpeas:
1 cup dried chickpeas (chole/chana), soaked overnight
3-4 cups water
1 bay leaf
2-3 black cardamom pods
1-inch cinnamon stick
1 teaspoon salt
For the Gravy:
2 tablespoons oil
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 large onion, finely chopped
2 green chilies, slit
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
2 large tomatoes, pureed
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1 teaspoon cumin powder
1 teaspoon garam masala
1 teaspoon chole masala (optional)
1 teaspoon dried mango powder (amchur)
Salt, to taste
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish
Lemon wedges, for serving

For the Chole Recipe:
Cooking Chickpeas:
Rinse the soaked chickpeas and drain the water. In a pressure cooker, add the chickpeas, 3-4 cups of water, bay leaf, black cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, and salt. Pressure cook for 15-20 minutes or until the chickpeas are soft and cooked through. If using a regular pot, simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours.

Preparing the Gravy:
Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add the finely chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown. Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 2-3 minutes until the raw smell goes away. Add the pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil starts to separate from the mixture. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.

Combining Chickpeas with Gravy:
Add the cooked chickpeas along with their cooking water to the gravy. Mix well. Simmer on low heat for about 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if needed. Add garam masala, chole masala (if using), and dried mango powder. Mix well and cook for another 5 minutes.

Serving:
Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with bhature, puri, roti, or rice. Serve with lemon wedges on the side for an extra tangy kick.

Bhature

For the Bhature Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
2 tablespoons semolina (sooji/rava)
1/2 cup yogurt (curd)
1 teaspoon sugar
1 teaspoon salt
1/2 teaspoon baking powder
1/4 teaspoon baking soda
2 tablespoons oil or ghee
Water, as needed for kneading
Oil, for deep frying

For the Bhature Recipe:
Preparing the Dough:
In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, semolina, sugar, salt, baking powder, and baking soda. Add the yogurt and mix well. Add oil or ghee and mix until the flour mixture resembles breadcrumbs. Gradually add water and knead the dough until it is smooth and soft. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 2-3 hours.

Rolling the Bhature:
After the dough has rested, divide it into small lemon-sized balls. Roll each ball into a smooth ball and flatten it slightly with your hands. On a lightly floured surface, roll out each ball into a circular shape about 5-6 inches in diameter. The thickness should be about 1/4 inch.

Frying the Bhature:
Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium-high heat. To check if the oil is hot enough, drop a small piece of dough into the oil. If it rises to the surface quickly and puffs up, the oil is ready. Carefully slide one rolled bhature into the hot oil. It should puff up immediately. Gently press the bhature with a slotted spoon to help it puff up fully. Fry until both sides are golden brown, then remove the bhature with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.

Serving:
Serve the hot and fluffy Bhature immediately with Chole, pickle, and onions on the side.

Lassi

For the Lassi Ingredients:

2 cups plain yogurt (curd)
1/2 cup cold water or milk
1/2 cup ice cubes (optional)
3-4 tablespoons sugar (adjust to taste)
1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
A pinch of salt (optional)
Chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios) and saffron strands for garnish (optional)

For the Lacci Recipe:
Blending the Ingredients:
In a blender, add the yogurt, cold water or milk, ice cubes (if using), sugar, and cardamom powder. Blend until smooth and frothy. Adjust the consistency by adding more water or milk if needed. Taste and adjust the sweetness by adding more sugar if desired. If you prefer a hint of salt, add a small pinch and blend again.

Serving:
Pour the lassi into glasses. Garnish with chopped nuts and a few saffron strands if desired.

Kulcha

For the Kulcha Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
1/2 teaspoon baking powder
1/4 teaspoon baking soda
1 teaspoon sugar
1 teaspoon salt
2 tablespoons yogurt (curd)
2 tablespoons oil or ghee
1/2 cup milk (or as needed for kneading)
Nigella seeds (kalonji) or sesame seeds, for topping (optional)
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (optional)

For the Kulcha Recipe:
Preparing the Dough:
In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, sugar, and salt. Add the yogurt and oil or ghee. Mix well. Gradually add milk and knead the dough until it is smooth and soft. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 1-2 hours.

Shaping the Kulcha:
After the dough has rested, divide it into small lemon-sized balls. Roll each ball into a smooth ball and flatten it slightly with your hands. On a lightly floured surface, roll out each ball into an oval or round shape about 6-7 inches in diameter and 1/4 inch thick. Sprinkle nigella seeds or sesame seeds and chopped coriander leaves on top of each kulcha and gently press them into the dough with a rolling pin.

Cooking the Kulcha:
Heat a tawa or griddle over medium-high heat. Place a rolled-out kulcha on the hot tawa. Cook until bubbles start to form on the surface. Flip the kulcha and cook until the other side is golden brown and cooked through. You can brush some butter or ghee on the cooked kulcha for extra flavor. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.

Serving:
Serve hot kulchas with chole, curry, or any side dish of your choice.

Paneer Butter Masala

For the Paneer Butter Masala Ingredients:
For the Paneer:
250 grams paneer, cubed
2 tablespoons butter
1 tablespoon oil
For the Gravy:
2 large tomatoes, pureed
1 large onion, finely chopped
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
2-3 green chilies, slit (optional)
1/2 cup fresh cream
1/4 cup cashews, soaked in warm water and ground to a paste
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1 teaspoon garam masala
1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
2 tablespoons tomato ketchup or 1 tablespoon tomato paste (optional, for color and tanginess)
Salt, to taste
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish

For the Paneer Butter Masala Recipe:
Preparing the Paneer:
In a pan, heat the butter and oil over medium heat. Add the paneer cubes and lightly fry them until golden brown. Remove and set aside.

Preparing the Gravy:
In the same pan, add a little more butter if needed. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies (if using). Sauté for another 2-3 minutes until the raw smell goes away. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil starts to separate from the mixture. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Add the cashew paste and cook for a few minutes until the gravy thickens and becomes creamy.

Combining Paneer with Gravy:
Add the tomato ketchup or tomato paste (if using) and mix well. Add the fried paneer cubes to the gravy and simmer for 5-7 minutes, allowing the paneer to absorb the flavors. Stir in the fresh cream and mix well. Crush the kasuri methi between your palms and add it to the gravy. Add garam masala and mix well. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, adjusting the seasoning as needed.

Serving:
Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
