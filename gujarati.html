Gujarati Recipes

Khandvi

For the Khandvi Ingredients:
For the Khandvi Batter:

1 cup gram flour (besan)
1 cup yogurt (curd)
1 cup water
1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
1/2 teaspoon ginger-green chili paste
Salt to taste
For the Tempering:
2 tablespoons oil
1 teaspoon mustard seed
1 teaspoon sesame seeds
A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
8-10 curry leaves
2-3 green chilies, slit
For Garnish:
Fresh grated coconut
Chopped coriander leaves

For the Khandvi Recipe:
Prepare the Batter:
In a mixing bowl, combine the gram flour, yogurt, water, turmeric powder, ginger-green chili paste, and salt.Whisk the mixture well to ensure there are no lumps.

Cook the Batter:
Pour the batter into a non-stick pan.Cook on low-medium heat, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.Continue cooking until the batter thickens and turns into a smooth, glossy paste (this takes about 10-12 minutes).

Test and Spread the Batter:
To check if the batter is ready, spread a small spoonful on a greased plate or surface. If it rolls up easily after cooling, it is ready.Quickly spread the batter thinly and evenly on the back of a greased plate or a large tray using a spatula or the back of a ladle.

Cut and Roll:
Once the spread batter cools down (it should take a few minutes), cut it into strips about 2 inches wide.Carefully roll each strip into tight rolls and place them on a serving plate.

Prepare the Tempering:
Heat oil in a small pan.Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.Add sesame seeds, asafoetida, curry leaves, and slit green chilies. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.

Garnish and Serve:
Pour the tempering evenly over the khandvi rolls.Garnish with fresh grated coconut and chopped coriander leaves.

Serve:
Serve the khandvi rolls as a snack or appetizer. Enjoy!

Dhokla

For the Dhokla Ingredients:
For the Dhokla Batter:
1 cup gram flour (besan)
1 cup yogurt (curd)
1/2 cup water (or as needed)
1 teaspoon ginger-green chili paste
1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 tablespoon lemon juice
1 teaspoon Eno fruit salt or 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
Salt to taste
For the Tempering:
2 tablespoons oil
1 teaspoon mustard seeds
1 teaspoon sesame seeds
A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
8-10 curry leaves
2-3 green chilies, slit
For Garnish:
Fresh grated coconut
Chopped coriander leaves

For the Dhokla Recipe:
Prepare the Batter:
In a mixing bowl, combine gram flour, yogurt, water, ginger-green chili paste, turmeric powder, lemon juice, and salt.Whisk the mixture well to ensure there are no lumps and to form a smooth batter. The batter should be of pouring consistency.

Prepare the Steamer:
Fill a large pot or steamer with a few inches of water and bring it to a boil.Grease a round steaming dish or thali with oil.
Add the Leavening Agent: Just before steaming, add Eno fruit salt or baking soda to the batter.Mix gently but thoroughly, making sure the batter becomes frothy.

Steam the Dhokla:
Pour the batter immediately into the greased steaming dish.Place the dish in the steamer and cover with a lid.Steam for about 15-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Prepare the Tempering:
Heat oil in a small pan.Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.Add sesame seeds, asafoetida, curry leaves, and slit green chilies. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.

Apply the Tempering:
Once the dhokla is done, remove it from the steamer and let it cool for a few minutes.Pour the prepared tempering evenly over the dhokla.

Cut and Garnish:
Cut the dhokla into squares or diamond shapes.Garnish with fresh grated coconut and chopped coriander leaves.

Serve:
Serve the dhokla warm or at room temperature with green chutney and/or sweet tamarind chutney.

Thepla

For the Thepla Ingredients:
For the Dough:
2 cups whole wheat flour
1/2 cup besan (gram flour)
1/2 cup finely chopped fresh fenugreek leaves (methi) or 2 tablespoons dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi)
1/4 cup yogurt (curd)
2 tablespoons oil
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder
1 teaspoon cumin powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
1 teaspoon sesame seeds (optional)
1 tablespoon ginger-green chili paste
Salt to taste
Water as needed for kneading
For Cooking:
Oil or ghee for frying

For the Thepla Recipe:
Prepare the Dough:
In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour, besan, chopped fenugreek leaves (or kasuri methi), yogurt, oil, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, carom seeds, sesame seeds (if using), ginger-green chili paste, and salt.Mix all the ingredients well.Gradually add water and knead into a soft and smooth dough. The dough should not be too stiff or too soft.Cover the dough and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.

Roll the Theplas:
Divide the dough into small, equal-sized balls.Dust each ball with a little flour and roll it out into a thin circle (about 6-7 inches in diameter) using a rolling pin. The thepla should be thin but not too thin that it tears.

Cook the Theplas:
Heat a tawa (griddle) or non-stick pan over medium heat.Place the rolled-out thepla on the hot tawa.Cook for a few seconds until you see bubbles forming on the surface. Flip the thepla.Apply a little oil or ghee on the cooked side and flip again.Apply oil or ghee on the other side as well. Press lightly with a spatula and cook until both sides are golden brown with some brown spots.Remove from the tawa and place on a plate.Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.

Serve:
Serve theplas hot or at room temperature with yogurt, pickle, or any chutney of your choice.

Undhiyu

For the Undhiyu Sak Ingredients:
For Methi Muthiyas:
1 cup fenugreek leaves (methi), finely chopped
1/2 cup gram flour (besan)
1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
1/2 teaspoon sugar (optional)
A pinch of baking soda
Salt to taste
1 tablespoon oil
Oil for deep frying
For the Vegetables:
1 cup baby potatoes, peeled and halved
1 cup sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
1 cup purple yam (ratalu), peeled and cubed
1 cup raw bananas, peeled and sliced
1 cup eggplants (brinjal), slit crosswise but kept whole
1/2 cup fresh or frozen surti papdi (flat green beans)
1/2 cup fresh or frozen pigeon peas (tuvar lilva)
1/2 cup fresh or frozen green peas
For the Masala Paste:
1 cup grated fresh coconut
1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
2 tablespoons ginger-green chili paste
1 teaspoon cumin powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
2 tablespoons jaggery or sugar (optional)
Salt to taste
1 tablespoon lemon juice
For Tempering:
1/4 cup oil
1 teaspoon mustard seeds
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
A pinch of asafoetida (hing) 10-12 curry leaves

For the Undhiyu Sak Recipe:
Prepare Methi Muthiyas:
In a mixing bowl, combine the chopped fenugreek leaves, gram flour, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, sugar, baking soda, salt, and 1 tablespoon oil.Mix well and form a dough. Add a little water if needed.Shape the dough into small oval dumplings (muthiyas).Heat oil in a deep frying pan and deep fry the muthiyas until golden brown and crisp. Drain on paper towels and set aside.

Prepare the Masala Paste:
In a blender, combine grated coconut, coriander leaves, ginger-green chili paste, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, jaggery or sugar, salt, and lemon juice.Blend to a coarse paste and set aside.

Prepare the Vegetables:
In a large mixing bowl, add all the prepared vegetables and mix them with half of the masala paste. Make sure the vegetables are well coated with the masala.

Tempering and Cooking Undhiyu:
In a large heavy-bottomed pan or pressure cooker, heat 1/4 cup oil.Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.Add cumin seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Sauté for a few seconds.Add the remaining masala paste and sauté for a couple of minutes. Add the masala-coated vegetables and mix gently.If using a pressure cooker, add 1/2 cup water, cover, and cook for 2 whistles. If using a regular pan, cover and cook on low heat, stirring occasionally until the vegetables are tender (this may take about 30-40 minutes). Add water as needed to prevent sticking. Once the vegetables are cooked, gently mix in the fried methi muthiyas.

Serve:
Transfer the undhiyu to a serving dish and garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with puris or rotis and enjoy!

Basundi

For the Basundi Ingredients:

1 liter full cream milk
1/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
A few strands of saffron (optional, soaked in 1 tablespoon warm milk)
2 tablespoons chopped almonds
2 tablespoons chopped cashews
2 tablespoons chopped pistachios
2 tablespoons chopped chironji (charoli) nuts
1 tablespoon raisins
2 tablespoons chopped dried figs (optional)
2 tablespoons chopped dried apricots (optional)
1 teaspoon rose water or 1/2 teaspoon rose essence (optional)

For the Basundi Recipe:
Boil the Milk:
Pour the milk into a heavy-bottomed pan or kadai and bring it to a boil over medium heat.Once the milk starts boiling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer.

Thicken the Milk:
Keep stirring the milk occasionally to prevent it from sticking to the bottom and sides of the pan.As the milk thickens and reduces, a layer of cream (malai) will form on the surface. Gently stir this cream back into the milk.Continue this process for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the milk is reduced to about half its original volume and has thickened.

Add Sugar and Flavorings:
Add the sugar to the thickened milk and stir well until it dissolves completely.Add the cardamom powder and saffron milk (if using). Mix well.

Add Dry Fruits:
Add the chopped almonds, cashews, pistachios, chironji nuts, raisins, dried figs, and dried apricots. Stir well to combine.Let the mixture simmer for another 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Finishing Touch:
If using rose water or rose essence, add it now and mix well.Remove the pan from heat and let the basundi cool to room temperature.

Serve:
Transfer the basundi to a serving bowl and garnish with a few strands of saffron and chopped nuts.You can serve basundi warm or chilled, according to your preference.

Jalebi

For the Jalebi Ingredients:
For the Batter:
1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
2 tablespoons cornstarch (optional, for extra crispiness)
1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder (for color) or a few drops of yellow/orange food coloring
1/2 cup plain yogurt
1/2 cup water (or as needed)
1/4 teaspoon baking powder or 1/8 teaspoon baking soda
For the Sugar Syrup:
1 1/2 cups sugar
3/4 cup water
1/4 teaspoon saffron threads (optional)
1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
1/2 teaspoon lemon juice
A few drops of rose water (optional)
For Frying:
Ghee or oil for deep frying

For the Jalebi Recipe:
Prepare the Batter:
In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, cornstarch (if using), turmeric powder (or food coloring), and baking powder (or baking soda).Add the yogurt and mix well.Gradually add water and whisk until you get a smooth, lump-free batter of pouring consistency.Cover the batter and let it ferment for at least 4-6 hours or overnight at room temperature.

Prepare the Sugar Syrup:
In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar dissolves. Once the sugar dissolves, add saffron threads (if using), cardamom powder, and lemon juice. Let the syrup simmer until it reaches a one-string consistency (when you touch the syrup between your thumb and forefinger, it should form a single thread). Remove from heat and add a few drops of rose water (if using). Keep the syrup warm.

Frying the Jalebis:
Heat ghee or oil in a wide, shallow pan over medium heat. Pour the batter into a squeeze bottle, piping bag, or a thick ziplock bag with a small hole cut at one corner. Squeeze the batter into the hot oil in a spiral shape, starting from the center and working outward. Fry a few jalebis at a time. Fry the jalebis until they are golden and crispy on both sides, turning occasionally with tongs.

Soak in Sugar Syrup:
Remove the fried jalebis from the oil and drain the excess oil. Immediately immerse the hot jalebis into the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for about 30 seconds to 1 minute. Remove the jalebis from the syrup and place them on a plate or tray.

Serve:
Serve the jalebis warm or at room temperature. They can be enjoyed on their own or with rabri (thickened sweetened milk) for an extra indulgent treat.
