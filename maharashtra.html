Maharashtrian Recipes

Pav Bhaji

For the Pav Bhaji Ingredients:

2 large potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
1 cup cauliflower florets, finely chopped
1/2 cup green peas
1 large capsicum (bell pepper), finely chopped
2-3 tomatoes, finely chopped
1 large onion, finely chopped
1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
2-3 tablespoons pav bhaji masala
Salt, to taste
Butter, as needed
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
Lemon wedges
Pav (bread rolls), lightly toasted with butter

For the Pav Bhaji Recipe:
Preparing the Bhaji:
Heat butter in a large pan or kadai over medium heat. Add ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, and onions. Sauté until onions turn translucent. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy. Add chopped capsicum, cauliflower, and green peas. Mix well. Add turmeric powder, pav bhaji masala, and salt. Mix everything together. Add mashed potatoes and mix thoroughly, mashing the vegetables with the back of a spoon or a potato masher. Add water as needed to adjust the consistency. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the bhaji is well cooked and flavors are blended.

Assembling Pav Bhaji:
Serve hot bhaji garnished with chopped coriander leaves, a dollop of butter, and lemon wedges. Serve toasted pav alongside.

Vada Pav

For the Vada Pav Ingredients:

For Potato Vada:
4 medium potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
1 teaspoon mustard seeds
1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 tablespoon lemon juice
A few curry leaves, chopped
Salt, to taste
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
For Batter:
1 cup chickpea flour (besan)
A pinch of baking soda
Salt, to taste
Water, as needed
Other Ingredients:
Pav (bread rolls), sliced
Green chutney or tamarind chutney
Dry garlic chutney (optional)
Butter, as needed

For the Vada Pav Recipe:
Preparing Potato Vada:
Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add cumin seeds, hing, green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, and curry leaves. Sauté for a minute. Add turmeric powder and mashed potatoes. Mix well. Add salt, lemon juice, and chopped coriander leaves. Mix thoroughly. Shape the mixture into small round balls and set aside.

Frying Vada:
Prepare a thick batter using chickpea flour, baking soda, salt, and water. The consistency should coat the back of a spoon. Dip each potato ball in the batter, ensuring it's well coated. Deep fry in hot oil until golden brown and crispy. Drain excess oil on paper towels.

Assembling Vada Pav:
Slice pav horizontally without cutting all the way through. Spread green chutney or tamarind chutney on one side and butter on the other side. Place a vada in the pav and sprinkle dry garlic chutney on top (if using). Serve hot with more chutney or ketchup.

Puranpoli

For the PuranPoli Ingredients:
For Puran (Sweet Filling):
1 cup chana dal (split Bengal gram)
1 cup jaggery or sugar
1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
A pinch of nutmeg powder (optional)
For Poli (Outer Dough):
1 cup whole wheat flour (atta)
Water, as needed
Ghee or oil, for cooking

For the Puranoli Recipe:
Preparing Puran:
Wash and pressure cook chana dal with enough water until soft and cooked. Drain excess water and mash the dal well. In a pan, heat jaggery or sugar with a little water until it melts and forms a syrup. Add mashed chana dal, cardamom powder, and nutmeg powder (if using). Cook until the mixture thickens and comes together. Let the puran mixture cool completely.

Preparing Poli:
Knead whole wheat flour with water to make a soft dough. Divide the dough and puran mixture into equal portions.

Assembling Puran Poli:
Take a portion of dough and roll it out into a small disc. Place a portion of puran mixture in the center. Gather the edges and seal the filling completely. Roll out gently into a thin disc, dusting with flour as needed to prevent sticking. Heat a tava (griddle) over medium heat. Cook the poli on both sides with ghee or oil until golden brown spots appear. Serve hot or warm with a dollop of ghee.

Sabudana Khichdi

For the Sabudana Khichdi Ingredients:

1 cup sabudana (tapioca pearls)
1 medium-sized potato, peeled and diced
2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter) or oil
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1/2 cup roasted peanuts, coarsely ground
1/2 teaspoon sugar (optional)
Salt to taste
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
Freshly grated coconut (optional, for garnish)
Lemon wedges (for serving)

For the Sabudana Khichdi Recipe:
Preparing Sabudana:
Rinse the sabudana under running water until it becomes clear. Drain the water completely. Soak the sabudana in just enough water to cover it, for about 4-5 hours or overnight. The sabudana should be soaked until it becomes soft and non-sticky.

Cooking:
Heat ghee or oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped green chilies and diced potatoes. Sauté until the potatoes are cooked and lightly browned. Add the soaked sabudana to the pan. Stir gently to mix well with the potatoes. Cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring frequently, until the sabudana pearls turn translucent and separate. Be gentle to avoid mashing them. Add ground peanuts, sugar (if using), and salt to taste. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and freshly grated coconut, if desired. Serve hot with lemon wedges on the side.

Modak

For the Modak Ingredients:

1 cup rice flour
1.25 cups water
A pinch of salt
1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)
1 cup grated fresh coconut
3/4 cup jaggery (grated or powdered)
1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
Ghee or oil, for greasing

For the Modak Recipe:
Preparing the Dough:
In a pan, bring water to a boil with a pinch of salt and 1 tablespoon of ghee. Lower the heat and gradually add rice flour, stirring continuously to prevent lumps. Mix well until the dough forms and comes together. Turn off the heat and cover the pan. Let it rest for 5-10 minutes.

Making the Filling:
Heat a separate pan and add grated coconut and jaggery. Cook on medium-low heat until the jaggery melts and mixes well with the coconut, forming a sticky mixture. Add cardamom powder for flavor and mix thoroughly. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

Shaping the Modaks:
Take a small portion of the dough and knead it gently. Divide it into small balls. Grease your palms with ghee or oil. Take one ball of dough and flatten it into a small disc. Place a spoonful of the coconut-jaggery filling in the center of the disc. Pinch the edges of the disc together to seal the filling inside, shaping it into a cone or a modak shape. Use your fingers to create pleats on the top if desired.

Steaming:
Prepare a steamer and grease the steaming plate or tray with ghee or oil. Place the modaks on the greased plate and steam them for about 10-12 minutes on medium heat until they are cooked through and shiny.

Serve:
Allow the modaks to cool slightly before removing them from the steamer. Offer them as prasad (offering) to Lord Ganesha during Ganesh Chaturthi or enjoy them as a sweet treat.

Kande Pohe

For the Kande Pohe Ingredients:
1 cup thick poha (flattened rice)
1 medium onion, finely chopped
1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
8-10 curry leaves
2 tablespoons roasted peanuts
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
Salt to taste
1 tablespoon oil
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish) Freshly grated coconut (optional, for garnish) Lemon wedges (for serving)

For the kande Pohe Recipe:
reparing Poha:
Rinse the poha under running water in a sieve or colander until it becomes soft. Drain all the water and let the poha sit for about 10 minutes to soften further.

Cooking:
Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add cumin seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant. Add finely chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.

Adding Poha:
Lower the heat and add turmeric powder and salt to the onions. Mix well. Add the soaked poha and gently mix everything together using a spatula. Be careful not to break the poha. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the poha is heated through and well combined with the spices.

Garnish and Serve:
Add roasted peanuts and mix well. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and freshly grated coconut, if using. Serve hot with lemon wedges on the side for squeezing over the pohe.
